We have presented \dart{}, a compact 0.75\,M-parameter defect-aware graph
transformer that achieves state-of-the-art accuracy for predicting defect
formation energies in two-dimensional materials.
Three principal findings emerge:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Accuracy with efficiency.}
    \dart{} reduces the test MAE on IMP2D from 0.540\eV{} (ALIGNN, 4.03\,M
    parameters) to 0.407\eV{} (single model) and 0.359\eV{} (ensemble),
    representing a 25--34\% improvement with 5.4$\times$ fewer parameters.

  \item \textbf{Quantified generalization boundaries.}
    The graduated three-tier OOD assessment reveals that \dart{} degrades
    gracefully within the TMD family (1.5$\times$ MAE increase under
    leave-one-host-out), with Mo-based hosts being systematically easier to
    predict than W-based hosts---a trend rooted in orbital delocalization
    differences between 4$d$ and 5$d$ electrons.

  \item \textbf{Calibrated uncertainty.}
    Temperature-scaled ensemble uncertainty achieves 93.4\% coverage at the
    90\% nominal level, enabling practitioners to identify unreliable
    predictions before deployment.
\end{enumerate}

Together, these results establish \dart{} as a reliable and efficient
surrogate for high-throughput screening of 2D material defect properties,
with clearly delineated applicability domains.
The code, trained models, and evaluation protocols are publicly available
to support reproducibility and community benchmarking.
