\title{DART: A Compact Defect-Aware Radial Transformer for High-Throughput Prediction of Defect Formation Energies in Two-Dimensional Materials}

\author{Yiming Hua}
\affiliation{Wuhan University, Wuhan, China}
% TODO: add other authors

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
Point defects control many electronic, optical, and catalytic properties
of two-dimensional (2D) materials, but first-principles formation-energy
calculations remain too expensive for exhaustive host--dopant screening.
Here we introduce \dart{} (Defect-Aware Radial Transformer), a compact
graph transformer for 2D defect formation energy prediction. \dart{}
combines local bond-angle message passing, global periodic self-attention,
and an explicit defect-site embedding, and is trained with a data-limited
recipe that emphasizes robust loss design, augmentation, and multi-source
representation learning.

On the IMP2D benchmark, \dart{} outperforms established graph-neural-network
baselines while using substantially fewer parameters, and ensemble averaging
further improves accuracy and uncertainty calibration. Ablation experiments
show that the gain comes not from model scaling alone, but from the coupling
of defect-aware inductive bias with careful training methodology. We further
evaluate transfer beyond random splits using leave-one-host-out and
compositional block-out protocols, showing that \dart{} degrades gracefully
within related 2D-material families while exposing clear limits for broader
extrapolation. These results position \dart{} as an efficient surrogate for
rapid defect screening and uncertainty-guided triage before targeted
first-principles validation.
\end{abstract}
