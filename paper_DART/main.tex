\title{DART: A Compact Defect-Aware Radial Transformer for High-Throughput Prediction of Defect Formation Energies in Two-Dimensional Materials}

\author{Yiming Hua}
\affiliation{Wuhan University, Wuhan, China}
% TODO: add other authors

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
Point defects critically influence the electronic, optical, and catalytic
properties of two-dimensional (2D) materials, yet first-principles
prediction of defect formation energies ($\ef$) across vast host--dopant
spaces remains prohibitively expensive.
%
Here we introduce \dart{} (Defect-Aware Radial Transformer), a compact
0.75\,M-parameter graph transformer that couples local bond-angle message
passing with global periodic self-attention, augmented by an explicit
defect-site embedding.
%
On the IMP2D benchmark (10\,641 impurity configurations across 44 host
monolayers), \dart{} achieves a test MAE of 0.407~eV---25\% below ALIGNN
with $5.4\times$ fewer parameters---with systematic ablation confirming
that training engineering accounts for the majority of the gain.
%
A graduated out-of-distribution assessment quantifies generalization
boundaries: leave-one-host-out extrapolation within the transition-metal
dichalcogenide family yields a mean MAE of 0.54~eV, accompanied by
calibrated ensemble uncertainty (93\% coverage at the 90\% nominal level).
%
These results demonstrate that physically informed inductive biases
combined with rigorous training methodology, rather than parameter
scaling, are the primary levers for accurate and trustworthy
defect-property surrogates in the data-limited materials regime.
\end{abstract}
